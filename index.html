Each mode uses techniques the cognitive-science literature ranks at the top for retention: active recall (you generate the answer), immediate feedback with explanation, spaced repetition (Leitner boxes — items you miss come back faster), interleaving (topics are mixed, not blocked), desirable difficulty (multi-select & numeric input rather than easy MC), and confidence calibration (rate yourself before you answer).

Why these techniques work

Roediger & Karpicke (2006) and Dunlosky et al. (2013) ranked practice testing and distributed practice as the two most effective learning strategies — far above re-reading or highlighting. This trainer combines both with the Leitner box system (Leitner, 1972), which puts more weight on items you struggle with. The confidence rating step is from Bjork's research on metacognition: forcing a self-prediction before the answer reveals where you're overconfident, which is exactly where exam mistakes come from.
